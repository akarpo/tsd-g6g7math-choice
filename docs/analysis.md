# Troy School District G6–G7 Math: Detracking + Illustrative Mathematics Analysis

## Executive Summary

**Question:** Has Troy School District's Fall 2023 adoption of Illustrative Mathematics and detracking of 6th and 7th grade math produced results that outperform its characteristics?

**Answer: No.** Three independent lines of evidence converge on the same conclusion:

1. **Troy ranks 197 of 295 level-matched peers (67th percentile, bottom third) in recovery during the exact IM adoption window (2022–23 → 2024–25).** Most similar districts recovered faster without IM or detracking.

2. **Troy's Asian students — 36% of enrollment and historically the district's strongest math performers — show the sharpest decline.** G7 Asian math actually *continued declining* (−0.094 grade levels) during the IM window when it should have been recovering.

3. **Every major detracking experiment with published data (San Francisco, Cambridge) has either been reversed or is being reversed** after widening rather than narrowing achievement gaps. Troy's early trajectory mirrors these patterns.

TSD's claim of "nearly 50% Algebra 1 enrollment" in the 2025–26 cohort is a course-access metric, not an achievement metric. The NAEP-anchored SEDA data shows that the students arriving at Algebra 1 are doing so at significantly lower math readiness than pre-COVID and pre-IM cohorts.

**Primary metric:** SEDA cs_mn — cohort-standardized mean. A score of **0.0** = national grade-level norm. **+1.0** = one full grade level above the national average. ## Analysis 1: Pre/Post-COVID Math Decline

### Troy among 296 level-matched peers

Troy's pre-COVID math position (+0.937 grade levels above national norm) placed it among the strongest math districts in the country. Post-COVID, it declined to +0.750.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Pre-COVID G6+G7 math (2017–19) | **+0.937** |
| Post-COVID G6+G7 math (2022–25) | **+0.750** |
| **Decline (Δ)** | **−0.187 grade levels** |
| Rank among 296 peers | **104 of 296 (35th percentile)** |

**Interpretation:** 65% of districts at Troy's pre-COVID level declined *more* than Troy. Troy's overall COVID decline is moderate — not the worst, but worse than median. The issue is not the COVID decline itself, but the *failure to recover* once IM was adopted.

### Absolute performance ranking: Troy fell 59 places

Beyond deltas, the *absolute leaderboard* tells a stark story. Troy dropped from **rank 76 to rank 135** among 296 level-matched peers — losing 59 positions:

Troy lost its **#1 ranking in Asian math** among MI peers — Northville now leads. Four of five subgroups dropped in the rankings. Only White students (from 6th to 5th) moved up, and only because other districts declined more.

Troy is 35.8% Asian — this subgroup historically drove the district's math rankings. The Asian subgroup is where the clearest signal of underperformance appears.

## Analysis 4: The Detracking Evidence Base

### San Francisco Unified — The Longest-Running Detracking Experiment

SFUSD eliminated Algebra 1 in 8th grade in 2014–15 — the most prominent detracking experiment in U.S. education.

**Results after 10+ years:**
- Overall math achievement: **flat to declining** (Grade 11 Smarter Balanced dropped 1 point, 2015–2019)
- Black-White gap: **expanded 15 points** (vs. 11 statewide)
- Hispanic-White gap: **expanded 31 points** (vs. 5 statewide)
- AP math enrollment: **declined 15%** (6 percentage points), driven by Asian/Pacific Islander declines
- The district's claimed reduction in Algebra 1 repeat rates was an artifact of **eliminating the passing requirement**, not improved learning

**SFUSD reversed course in March 2026**, restoring Algebra 1 to all middle schools starting 2026–27 after a 12-year hiatus.

### Cambridge, MA

Cambridge detracked middle school math between 2017–2019. Results:
- Early reports showed some positive signs
- Immediately followed by COVID, obscuring signal
- **Cambridge is now reversing course**, restoring algebra to 8th grade, citing persistent achievement gaps

### Brookings Institution Assessment (2024)

> "Research literature is usually described as 'mixed,' but studies of *positive* effects resulting from detracking are scant."

A 2023 RAND survey found 39% of middle schools still track math — the majority view is that differentiation, not uniformity, serves students best.

## Analysis 5: The "50% Algebra 1" Claim

TSD claims nearly 50% of the 2025–26 cohort is enrolled in Algebra 1 — presented as evidence of success.

This metric is **misleading for three reasons:**

1. **Course enrollment ≠ course mastery.** SFUSD made the identical claim — more students in advanced courses — which collapsed under scrutiny when test scores showed no improvement and the passing requirement had simply been eliminated.

2. **The SEDA data shows the students reaching Algebra 1 are significantly less prepared** than previous cohorts. Troy's G6+G7 math cs score dropped from +0.937 (2017–19) to +0.750 (2022–25) — a quarter-grade-level decline in readiness. Putting less-prepared students into Algebra 1 may produce an enrollment statistic without producing Algebra 1-level competence.

3. **The relevant comparison is not past Troy enrollment; it's what peer districts achieve.** High-performing Asian-demographic peers like Bellevue, Issaquah, Coppell, and West Windsor-Plainsboro all offer accelerated math pathways *with tracking*. Their Asian and overall math scores have recovered faster than Troy's.

## Analysis 6: Illustrative Mathematics — What the Evidence Shows

### Published IM research

| Study | Finding | Relevance |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **NYC District 11** (Bronx) | Proficiency rose 25.8% → 50.6% (2022–2025) | High-poverty, low-baseline context — not comparable to Troy's affluent, high-baseline setting |
| **Fort Zumwalt SD** (MO) | Small-to-medium effect size (0.16 SD) | Effect size is modest; comparable to general instructional improvement, not transformative |
| **Philadelphia SD** | Year 2 implementation study (no outcome data yet) | Process study, not achievement evidence |
| **Uncommon Schools** | Adopted IM Fall 2024 — no outcomes yet | Too early for evidence |

**The critical gap:** There is no published evidence of IM producing outsized math gains in an *affluent, high-performing, high-Asian-enrollment* suburban district. All published IM success stories come from high-poverty, low-baseline contexts where any structured curriculum would likely show gains over what preceded it.

## Conclusions

### 1. Troy has NOT outperformed its characteristics

By every available metric — SEDA cross-state scores, level-matched peer ranking, MI peer comparison, IM-window recovery — Troy's math trajectory under IM + detracking is **below median** and often in the **bottom third** of comparable districts.

### 2. The Asian subgroup signal is most concerning

Troy's 36% Asian student population — the demographic most affected by detracking — shows continued decline in G7 math even during the IM adoption window. High-Asian peer districts with tracking (Bellevue, Issaquah) show robust recovery.

### 3. The detracking evidence base is against this approach

The two largest, longest-running detracking experiments (SFUSD, Cambridge) have both been reversed. Neither achieved its equity goals; both widened gaps. Troy is replicating a strategy with a well-documented failure pattern.

### 4. Course enrollment metrics are not achievement metrics

TSD's "50% Algebra 1" claim follows the exact playbook that SFUSD used for a decade before test scores revealed the emperor had no clothes. The SEDA data shows the students entering Algebra 1 are arriving with less math readiness than any recent Troy cohort.

### 5. The return on investment is negative

The question is not just whether IM + detracking is working, but whether it's working *better than the alternative*. Three MI peers (Birmingham, Bloomfield Hills, Northville) recovered significantly more math ground in the same period, in the same state, with the same test, while maintaining tracking. The opportunity cost of IM + detracking is measured by the gap between Troy's +0.024 recovery and what tracked peers achieved (+0.082 to +0.211).
